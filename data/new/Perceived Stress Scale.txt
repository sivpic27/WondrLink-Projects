Perceived Stress Scale  
 Why it’s considered the gold standard:
Developed by Cohen, Kamarck & Mermelstein (1983)
Measures perceived stress (how unpredictable, uncontrollable, and overloaded life feels)
Validated across:
General adult populations
Medical patients
Cancer populations
International translations
Strong internal consistency (Cronbach α typically 0.78–0.91)
Correlates with:
Cortisol levels
Immune biomarkers
Depression/anxiety scales
Cardiovascular outcomes
📋 Versions Available
👉 PSS-10 is the most widely validated and recommended version.
📊 What It Measures
It assesses how often in the last month someone felt:
Upset by unexpected events
Unable to control important things
Nervous or stressed
Confident in handling problems (reverse scored)
That difficulties were piling up
Score range (PSS-10): 0–40
0–13 → Low stress
14–26 → Moderate stress
27–40 → High stress
Other Highly Validated Stress Scales
2. Depression Anxiety Stress Scales (DASS-21 Stress Subscale)
21 total items (7 stress items)
Often used in clinical psychology
Measures physiological arousal and tension
3. Holmes and Rahe Stress Scale
Measures life event stress load
Less about perceived stress; more about objective stress exposure
📌 Clinical & Research Recommendation
For:
Clinical screening
Cancer populations
Community health screening apps
Longitudinal tracking
